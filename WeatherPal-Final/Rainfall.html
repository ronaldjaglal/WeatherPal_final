Rain is liquid water in the form of droplets that have condensed from atmpospheric water vapor and then become heavy enough to fall under gravity or precipitate

Facts about rainfall

  • Rain is a major component of the water cycle and is responsible for deposting most of the fresh water on the Earth.
  • The least rainy place on Earth is Actually Antarctica which only gets 6.5 inches of rain or snow per year.
  • The most rainy place in comparison is Lloro Colombia with more than 500 inches per year.
  • Rain doesn't always make the ground wet, In dry, hot places, rain sometimes evaporates before it hits the ground. Environmentalist Edward Abbey describes "phantom rain" this way: "You see curtains of rain dangling in the sky while the living things wither below for want of water. Torture by tantalizing, hope without fulfillment. Then the clouds dissipate into nothingness."
  • Not all raindrops are made of water, On Venus, and other moons and planets, rain is made of sulfuric acid or methane. Even stranger: On a planet 5,000 light years away, scientists found raindrops made of iron rather than water.
  • Water doesn't smell like anything, so why does rain produce a pleasant aroma after it falls? Well, it's because of a molecule, called geosmin, created by soil-dwelling bacteria. When rain falls, it creates air pockets, which contain small amounts of geosmin. The rain traps and then releases these air pockets, dispersing geosmin into the air, where it's free to travel to human sniffers. The smell of rain even has a name: "Petrichor."

    • How do we Measure Rainfall?

    The most common rainfall measurement is the total rainfall depth during a given period, expressed in millimeters (mm). This is done by using a rain gauge.
